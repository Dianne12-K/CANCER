African Forests Story Map

1. West Africa - Ankasa Conservation Area, Ghana

The Ankasa Conservation Area is a protected area in Ghana, known for its dense rainforest, rich biodiversity, and pristine environment. It is a crucial habitat for many rare and endangered species, including the forest elephant, bongo, and Diana monkey.

2. Central Africa - Congo Rainforest, Democratic Republic of Congo

The Congo Rainforest is the second-largest tropical rainforest in the world, playing a critical role in regulating the global climate. It is home to a vast array of wildlife, including gorillas, chimpanzees, and forest elephants, and supports the livelihoods of millions of people.

4. Southern Africa - Knysna Forest, South Africa

The Knysna Forest is an ancient forest in South Africa, known for its rich biodiversity and towering trees. It has a mystical allure, often associated with the elusive Knysna elephants. The forest also supports a variety of plant and animal species, making it a crucial conservation area.

5. North Africa - Middle Atlas Forest, Morocco

The Middle Atlas Forest in Morocco is characterized by its unique cedar forests. This region is crucial for maintaining local biodiversity and is the habitat for the endangered Barbary macaque. The forest is also important for the local communities who rely on its resources.
